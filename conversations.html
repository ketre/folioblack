Project Brief

To listen and record a variety of conversations, dialogue, comments that you hear outside of the college. Listen carefully and record what is heard or misheard as accurately as possible, asking yourself if is there's an accent, is the voice soft, loud, quiet or harsh? All notes to be transcribed and reproduced in a series of 4 of these voices of London as: A series of postcards start by using only type and two colours.

My process

This project was a one-day workshop to view typography as the driving force in the form of visual communication. I've experimented with typogrpahic layots and semantics with the use of static vs dynamic compositions, negative space, and chaotic vs organised alignment. Different techniques produced a result which is associated with the respective tones of voice and the emotional characteristic of the recorded phrase. Various font sizes and weights were used to communicate the implied hierarchy of the words in question, making them feel 'alive'.
